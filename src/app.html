Soccer: Shots and Passes

Our design helps the viewer visualize shot location and conversion on a regulation sized soccer goal post.

Each shot is denoted by a point drawn on the goalpost.
Red points are used to visualize shots that ended up in goal, while
Black points are used for shots that were saved or were off-target.

The opacity of a point denotes its xG statistic. xG, or eXpectedGoals, is a popular metric used to measure the likelihood of scoring from a shot.
Easier chances have higher xG (more solid), while harder chances have lower xG (more transparent).

You can start by selecting a team. By default all available data is displayed, but you can choose to filter based on season (year) and shot type.

The chart on the right corresponds with the team/season/shot type chosen and shows the number of shots that have certain characteristics.

Time = minute interval that the shot was made during the game

Technique = how the shot was made

Body Part = what part of a body was used to make the shot
